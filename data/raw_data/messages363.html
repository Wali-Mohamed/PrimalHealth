Depends on the specific metal, depends on the food type and duration of exposure. My opinion is that plastic ball jar lids are better in most cases of general food storage; they will leech very very small amounts of toxins if they're immersed but they will not corrode, flake and infuse the food with enamel and iron like the metal lids will once they get a little damaged. The regular metal canning lids are designed to be used once and discarded, specifically for canning. When we use them as daily-use lids they invariably get scratched and corrode rather rapidly, especially if the food is at all acidic, like fermented dairy. The ideal lid would be glass with a natural rubber seal, like the various bail-type jars and bottles that look old-fashioned. These are truly meant to be reused and are plastic free with the right washers. Otherwise, like I said, I'm partial to the plastic lids for daily-use. The only time I use metal ball jar lids is to give something away or as a dust cover on fermenting diary, being careful not to expose the lid to any of the liquid. YMMV. üôè
